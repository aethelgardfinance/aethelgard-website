# Glossary — how Aethelgard defines its terms

Used to keep cross-channel language consistent. When in doubt, defer to these definitions.

---

## Product terms

- **Vault** — the encrypted SQLite file Aethelgard stores the user's data in. Singular per user.
- **Local-first** — runs on the user's device; no cloud sync unless the user configures their own WebDAV target.
- **Tamper-evident** — every journal entry is SHA-256 linked to the previous one; modifying historical data breaks the chain.
- **Period lock** — closing a financial period so prior entries cannot be edited; standard professional practice.
- **Hash chain** — the cryptographic linkage of every entry to its predecessor.

## Audience terms

- **HNW** — High-Net-Worth. We use it precisely: typically £1M+ liquid investable assets, often multi-entity.
- **Family principal** — the financial steward of a multi-generation family's wealth. Often female, often inherited responsibility, not always wealthy by their own creation.
- **Family office** — formal multi-employee structure managing one family's wealth (single-family) or several (multi-family). We address sub-£50M structures *without* a formal SFO.
- **Founder-operator** — someone who built and ran the business they own; not a passive investor.

## Channel terms

- **Pillar** — one of four content categories: technical authority, founding story, succession, contrarian.
- **Seed** — Cherie's weekly Monday input; the only piece of content she creates from scratch.
- **Cornerstone** — a long-form (2000–3500 word) blog post built to rank for SEO and earn long-term.
- **Multiplier** — Ring 2 ICP; partners who recommend us, not buy from us.
