# Ideal Customer Profile — Aethelgard

Three concentric rings. Build everything for Ring 1. Use Ring 2 as multipliers. Ring 3 buys at full price and shouldn't be ignored, but isn't the centre.

---

## Ring 1 — The Bullseye

Build the product, the messaging, and the entire content strategy for these two personas.

### Persona 1A — The Founder-Operator with an Exit (or Mid-Exit)

| Attribute | Profile |
|---|---|
| Age | 38–58 |
| Geography | UK / NL / DE / Channel Islands / Singapore / UAE |
| Trigger event | Sold (or selling) a business; significant RSU/option vesting; 5–25 years compounding from a successful indie business |
| Net worth | £3M–£40M |
| Why not on Addepar | Below the £200M+ threshold where institutional platforms make sense |
| Operates | 1 holdco + 1–3 SPVs + ISA/SIPP + 1–4 properties + maybe carry/founder shares + multi-currency cash |
| Currently uses | Excel; Xero (badly, for the holdco); a bank dashboard; their head |
| Pays accountant | £15K–£60K/year and *still* can't answer "what do I own, in one place, today?" |
| Pain | Structure has outgrown spreadsheets; advisor sees fragments not the whole; can't model decisions confidently |
| Tier they buy | **Sovereign** or **Lifetime** |
| Hook | *"Stop bookkeeping in Excel for a £10M structure."* |

### Persona 1B — The Family Principal (sub-£50M, no formal SFO)

| Attribute | Profile |
|---|---|
| Status | Inherited or built; often the financial steward of a multi-generation family |
| Often (not always) | Female |
| Has | A "trusted advisor" but feels they don't fully control the picture |
| Acutely aware of | The succession problem |
| Tier they buy | **Sovereign** or **Corporate** |
| Hook | *"The conversation that should happen while it can happen."* |

## Ring 2 — The Multipliers

Don't sell *to* them. Sell *with* them. The trade is a free Aethelgard licence (Lifetime, ideally) in exchange for *one introduction* to a Ring 1 client.

| Type | Where to find |
|---|---|
| Koninklijke NBA peers (NL) | Cherie's existing network, NBA member directories |
| Small-practice ICAEW / ACCA (UK) HNW practitioners | LinkedIn, ICAEW community, regional accountancy networks |
| STEP-qualified trust practitioners | STEP UK & EMEA chapters |
| Boutique IFAs serving HNW | LinkedIn, Citywire New Model Adviser, Money Marketing |
| Specialist tax solicitors | LinkedIn, STEP, niche HNW law firms |

**Year 1 target: 5 strategic partners.** Not 50.

---

## Ring 3 — The Privacy-Weighted Buyer

Smaller volume, higher conversion at full price. Often arrive via niche communities and word-of-mouth, not paid acquisition.

- Crypto wealth holders who genuinely cannot put net-worth statements on US-server SaaS
- HK→UK / RU→EU / MENA expat wealth holders with sovereignty concerns
- Privacy-maxi tech founders
- Anyone with a structural reason to keep wealth data off cloud servers

**The Sovereign tier exists primarily for them.**

---

## Anti-ICP — who is NOT a customer

Be ruthless about saying no to these audiences. Pursuing them dilutes positioning and corrupts the content strategy.

| Audience | Why not |
|---|---|
| YNAB-happy users | Wrong job; we are not a budgeting app |
| PAYE / W-2 only earners with no entities | No structural complexity; Aethelgard is overkill |
| "Does it sync with my bank?" askers | Wrong frame — local-first means no aggregator integrations by design |
| £200M+ family offices | They have Addepar; we're not in that fight |
| Hobbyist accountants | Not the audience |
| Crypto degens (vs. crypto wealth holders) | Looking for tax-loss tools, not a serious ledger |

## How to recognise the ICP in the wild

Signals to look for in LinkedIn comments, replies, DMs, and inbound emails:

✅ **Signals of ICP fit**
- Mentions multiple entities, currencies, jurisdictions
- Mentions an accountant by name (relationship, not transaction)
- Asks about consolidation, hedge accounting, deferred tax — *or* mentions never having proper records
- References succession, executors, inheritance, or "what happens if I'm hit by a bus"
- Privacy framing — "I don't want my data on US servers"
- Uses specific terms: SPV, holdco, carry, founder shares, FX revaluation, period lock

❌ **Signals of anti-ICP**
- Asks about budgeting, expense categorisation, monthly bills
- Asks about bank sync, Plaid, Open Banking aggregation
- Asks if it works on iPhone
- Wants free / freemium / "do you have a free tier?"
- Comparing to YNAB, Monarch, Copilot
