## Why each credential matters

- **Koninklijke NBA** — the Royal Netherlands Institute of Chartered Accountants. The current professional body following the 2013 merger of NIVRA and NOvAA. Equivalent in standing to ICAEW (UK) or AICPA (US). Signals technical depth, regulated profession, ethical obligations. Critical for European ICP — particularly NL, DE, UK.
- **Drs Economics, Erasmus University Rotterdam** — Drs is a pre-Bologna Dutch master's-level title (equivalent to MSc). Erasmus is a top-tier European business school. Signals intellectual rigour beyond accounting craft.
- **Post-graduate Registered Accountant qualification** — the Dutch RA route, taught at Erasmus University Rotterdam (1995–1999). The professional qualification underpinning Koninklijke NBA membership.

> **Grade discipline (2026-04-28):** Cherie's instruction is that *no degree-grade qualifier appears in any byline or marketing copy*. The professional qualification (Registered Accountant) is the load-bearing credential; appending grade-of-degree language reads as overclaim and weakens not strengthens. LinkedIn no longer shows grade qualifiers either — staying aligned.
- **Twenty-five years** — long enough to have seen multiple market cycles, regulatory regimes, and the full life-arc of family wealth.
- **Finance and wealth management** — the canonical experience description on LinkedIn About. We say this verbatim. Concrete past roles include senior planning and analysis in higher education, media, and private client advisory — referenceable when context warrants but not the headline claim.

## What we explicitly do NOT claim

- ❌ **CPA** (US qualification — not held)
- ❌ **ACA / ACCA / FCA** (UK qualifications — not held; Cherie's LinkedIn does note an ACA-equivalence on the RA qualification, but ACA itself is the UK ICAEW credential and Cherie does not hold that)
- ❌ **NIVRA** (historical body name; superseded by Koninklijke NBA in 2013 — *do not use NIVRA in current materials*)
- ❌ **Big-4 partner** (not held)
- ❌ **CFA** (not held)
- ❌ **Chartered Financial Planner** (not held)
- ❌ Any "X under 30" / "Y under 40" press claims unless explicitly true and recent
- ❌ Anything about specific clients, AUM advised, or named family offices — confidentiality is part of the brand

## Confidentiality posture

**Cherie has worked with HNW clients, family offices, and complex structures for twenty-five years.** Aethelgard exists because she experienced the tooling gap firsthand. She does not name clients. She does not tell stories that could be reverse-engineered to identify a specific family or entity.

Anonymised, *composite* vignettes ("a client arrived last week with three folders…") are fine and powerful. They are the bedrock of the founding-story content pillar. Specific identifiers — even oblique ones (industry, geography, family size, asset class) — should always be obscured unless the client has explicitly consented.

This is not a marketing rule. It is a professional one. The Koninklijke NBA membership carries duties.
