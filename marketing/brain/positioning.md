# Positioning — Aethelgard

The single spine sentence, the three audience pivots, and the message ladder. **Do not deviate.**

---

## The spine sentence

> *Professional-grade accounting for serious wealth — built by an accountant, runs on your machine, answers the question your successor will eventually ask.*

Every piece of public-facing communication ladders up to that sentence. If a draft can't trace back to it, the draft is wrong.

---

## The three audience pivots

The ICP is segmented; the message is not. One spine, three angles of attack.

### Pivot A — Founders / Operators
**Lead with operational clarity.**
> *"Stop bookkeeping in Excel for a £10M structure."*

Used when the audience is founder-operators with exits, holdcos, SPVs, multi-currency cash. Speaks to control, decision quality, and outgrowing tools.

### Pivot B — Family Principals
**Lead with succession.**
> *"At some point — for all of us — someone else will need to understand what we own."*

Used for family principals, multi-generation stewards, anyone whose pain is legacy not operations. Emotional, never preachy.

### Pivot C — Privacy Buyers
**Lead with sovereignty.**
> *"Your net worth is the most sensitive document about you. It shouldn't live on someone else's server."*

Used for crypto wealth, expat wealth, privacy-maxi tech founders. Architectural, specific, never paranoid-sounding.

## Message ladder

When writing any piece, work from top to bottom. Each rung supports the one above.

| Rung | What goes here |
|---|---|
| 1. Spine | The single sentence above |
| 2. Pivot | One of A / B / C — pick based on audience and channel |
| 3. Proof | Founder credentials (Koninklijke NBA, Drs Economics, 25 years), the architectural choices (AES-256, SHA-256, local-first), or specific feature competence (IFRS 9, deferred tax, consolidation) |
| 4. Story | A specific human moment — a client conversation, a failed spreadsheet, a succession event. Always anonymised. |
| 5. Action | What we want the reader to do — read the next post, sign up to the newsletter, download, apply for design partner |

## Things we always say

- *"Local-first, by architectural choice"* — never just "private" or "secure"
- *"Built by a Registered Accountant (Koninklijke NBA)"* — credentials are central, not modest
- *"Your data never leaves your device"* — and we explain why architecturally, not as a marketing claim
- *"Double-entry accounting"* — technical correctness is the brand
- *"The conversation that should happen while it can happen"* — succession framing
- *"Professional-grade accounting for individuals"* — claims the gap explicitly

---

## Things we never say

- "Powered by AI" / "AI-driven" / any AI-feature claims about Aethelgard core
- "Bank-level security" — meaningless, we say what we actually do
- "Game-changer", "revolutionary", "disrupting" — empty
- "Easy to use" — we don't compete on simplicity, we compete on correctness
- "Built for everyone" — we are explicitly not for everyone
- "Affordable" — we are not the cheap option, we are the right option
- "Sync with your bank" — we don't, by design
- Any claim that compares us to YNAB / Monarch / Mint — wrong category
- Apologies for being Windows-only — it's a sequencing decision, not a limitation

---

## Tone guardrails

- **Authoritative, not arrogant.** The credentials speak; we don't shout.
- **Specific, not vague.** "AES-256, SHA-256 hash chain" beats "secure."
- **Plainspoken about complexity.** The audience knows what hedge accounting is; we don't define it. The audience does *not* know what local-first means; we explain it precisely.
- **Honest about edges.** Windows-only at launch. No mobile. No bank sync. We name these — they are features for our user, not gaps.
- **Never preachy on succession.** It is the most powerful frame we have, and the most easily ruined by overuse. Reach for it sparingly. Let it land.
