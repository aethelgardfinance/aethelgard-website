## Brian Bacon

**Status:** Nominated by Cherie 2026-04-28.

**Who:** Long-form LinkedIn Pulse author writing on leadership, ethics, and institutional moral dilemmas. Founder/chairman territory; addresses C-suite.

**Sample piece:** *"The US Draft is Back — It Has a Moral Question for Every Leader"*
https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/us-draft-back-has-moral-question-every-leader-brian-bacon-ifroc

### Stylistic features (distilled)

| Feature | What he does | Quoted / paraphrased example |
|---|---|---|
| Opening move | Blunt declarative proposition tied to a current event, then pivots to universal principle | *"The Draft is Back."* |
| Sentence rhythm | Varied; short declaratives punctuate longer reflective sentences | Short → long → short cadence |
| Vocabulary register | Plainspoken-elevated; everyday words mixed with philosophical terms (fiduciary, institutional pressure, moral architecture) without jargon-loading | "a directive comes down" alongside "institutional pressure" |
| Eastern / classical reference | Uses Tao Te Ching, Stoics, etc., as anchors — never as decoration | Light touch, not academic |
| Em-dashes | For expansion of an idea, not as comma substitute | One per paragraph at most |
| Italics | For the *one* word that must land | Sparingly |
| Lists | Bulleted parallel constructions to expose moral failure modes with clinical clarity | Three instinctive responses → each shown as a trap |
| Recurring move 1: pattern recognition | Names three instinctive responses, dismantles each | Compliance / rage / paralysis |
| Recurring move 2: reframing | Dresses compliance, rage, or paralysis in false legitimacy before exposing the cost | "looks like loyalty but is actually…" structure |
| Recurring move 3: interrogative pressure | Returns to probing questions rather than prescriptions | Doesn't tell the reader what to do |
| Stance on hard topics | Neutral framing ("I am not writing about whether X is right or wrong") then pivot to psychological/moral dimension | Avoids political tribe-signalling |
| Closing move | Rhetorical ultimatum framed as a question; existential stakes | *"If you have not yet decided what is worth more than your position, your institution has already decided for you."* |

### What to BORROW for Aethelgard

- **The opening move** — blunt declarative tied to a current/observable thing, then pivot to principle. Maps perfectly to Pillar 4 (contrarian) and Pillar 1 (technical authority).
- **Sentence rhythm** — short → long → short. Already partly in Cherie's About-page voice.
- **Eastern/classical reference, sparingly** — Cherie can do this with accounting and stewardship traditions (fiduciary duty, the steward concept, Roman pater familias, Dutch koopman tradition). Use rarely; never as decoration.
- **The "name three traps" structure** — particularly powerful for contrarian content (e.g. "three things founders try when their structure outgrows Excel — each a trap").
- **Closing as ultimatum-question** — *"If you cannot answer that question yourself, in one place, today, your successor will answer it for you, badly."* — this is **already** Cherie's instinct on the About page. Lean in.
- **Neutral framing on hard topics** — for any post touching politics-adjacent topics (HMRC, regulation, geopolitics, succession, mortality), this stance protects the brand. Borrow it explicitly.

### What NOT to BORROW

- **Bacon's metaphysical register** — he writes about *moral architecture* and *institutional soul*. Aethelgard writes about **technical correctness** and **architectural choice**. Different categories of rigour.
- **Long Pulse-style essays as the LinkedIn default** — he can carry 1500 words on Pulse; we keep LinkedIn posts under 1300 chars unless deliberately long-form.
- **Ethics-as-product framing** — Bacon sells leadership coaching; ethics is the centre. For Aethelgard, ethics shows up *through* the architecture (local-first, encrypted, no telemetry) — never as the headline claim.

### Composition cue for Claude

When drafting in Brian-Bacon-influenced mode, the structure is:

1. **Blunt declarative opener** tied to a real thing
2. **Pivot** — "I am not here to tell you whether X is right or wrong. I am here to tell you what it costs."
3. **Pattern recognition** — name three responses people instinctively reach for
4. **Dismantle each** — with one specific consequence
5. **Reframe** — what the mature response looks like
6. **Closing question** — existential, unresolved, the reader's to answer

This works best for Pillar 3 (succession) and Pillar 4 (contrarian). Less suited to Pillar 1 (technical authority — too philosophical) and Pillar 2 (founding story — too structured to feel personal).

## Mohamed El-Erian

**Status:** Nominated by Cherie 2026-04-28. *"I generally appreciate"* — calibrated positive reference.

**Who:** Economist and former PIMCO co-CIO; daily LinkedIn poster on macro / central banks / markets. Authoritative-but-approachable register; reaches institutional and informed-retail audience simultaneously.

**Sample piece:** Bank of Japan policy hold note — short LinkedIn post (~80 words) summarising the 6-3 vote, the dissenters, and the revised growth/inflation forecasts.

### Stylistic features (distilled)

| Feature | What he does | Quoted example |
|---|---|---|
| Length | Short — typically 60–120 words on macro updates | The whole BoJ post is one screen, no scroll |
| Opening move | Frames the news against expectation in the first clause | *"In line with market expectations, the Bank of Japan…"* |
| Structural move | Two-act: (1) what happened, (2) what was *more interesting* than the headline | *"More interesting, perhaps, was the shift in…"* |
| Hedging language | Calibrated, not weasel — "perhaps", "more interesting" — signals judgment without overclaiming | *"More interesting, perhaps,"* |
| Specificity | Always quantified — exact vote counts, exact percentage changes | *"6-3 vote"*, *"halved from 1% to 0.5%"*, *"revised upward from 1.9% to 2.8%"* |
| Vocabulary register | Plainspoken-technical: "stagflationary winds", "growth projection", "inflation outlook" — never academic, never dumbed down | Trusts the reader |
| Tone | Cool, observant, never partisan or alarmist | Names the Middle East War as a force, no editorial colour |
| No call to action | Doesn't tell the reader what to do; doesn't ask for engagement; doesn't link out | The information *is* the post |
| Hashtag use | Tasteful — 5 topic hashtags grouped at the end, not stuffed inline | `#economy #growth #inflation #japan #boj #markets` |
| No formatting tricks | No bullets, no emojis, no bold, no caps, no "👇" | Pure prose; gravitas through plainness |

### What to BORROW for Aethelgard

- **The two-act structure** — "Headline thing happened. *More interesting* was X." This is gold for Pillar 1 (technical authority) and Pillar 4 (contrarian) — it lets Cherie comment on a market/regulatory event while reframing what actually matters. Example: *"HMRC published the latest Trust Registration Service stats today. More interesting, perhaps, was the rise in non-UK-resident settlor registrations…"*
- **Quantified specificity** — every claim has a number. Cherie's accounting register makes this natural; the system should flag any draft that makes a claim without a number behind it.
- **Calibrated hedging** — *"perhaps"*, *"more interesting"*, *"arguably"* — these signal judgment without overclaiming, and they read as confidence not weakness. Borrow.
- **Cool, observant tone on hard topics** — when Aethelgard touches geopolitics-adjacent material (sanctions, FX volatility, regulatory shifts), El-Erian's register is the model. No editorial colour, no alarm. Let the facts carry the weight.
- **Tasteful hashtag stack** — 5–7 topic hashtags grouped at the end of LinkedIn posts. Acceptable. Stop short of inline keyword-stuffing.
- **Information *is* the post** — no CTA, no "what do you think", no engagement-bait. For Pillar 1 posts especially, this is the right model. Authority earned by *being right and saying it plainly* > authority sought by asking.
- **Short length** — 60–120 words proves a point doesn't always need 1000. Mix short El-Erian-style updates into the cadence, especially around UK Budget, HMRC announcements, IFRS amendments, BoE decisions, FCA notices.

### What NOT to BORROW

- **News-reaction-only cadence** — El-Erian's brand is built on daily macro reaction. That's not Aethelgard's brand. We post news-reactions occasionally as a *seasoning*, not as the main course. The four-pillar rotation still rules.
- **No-CTA-ever** — El-Erian doesn't need to drive trials; we sometimes do. For non-news posts we still close with intent (download, design partner, newsletter, or deliberate silence).
- **Macro-economist register** — he can talk BoJ and BoE; Aethelgard talks the *consequences* of those decisions for HNW individuals and family structures. We translate, we don't predict.

### Composition cue for Claude — "El-Erian mode"

Triggered when: a real, dateable event in the financial / regulatory / accounting world is worth commenting on (HMRC, BoE, FCA, IFRS Foundation, Companies House, Koninklijke NBA, FRC, Trust Registration Service, OECD/CRS, Pillar Two, etc.).

Structure (60–120 words, no formatting):

1. **Sentence 1** — frame the event against expectation: *"In line with [what was expected] / Against most expectations,"*
2. **Sentence 2–3** — what actually happened, with at least two numbers
3. **Sentence 4** — the pivot: *"More interesting, perhaps, was…"*
4. **Sentence 5–6** — what was actually more interesting, with one specific consequence for the Aethelgard reader (founder, family principal, multi-entity holder)
5. **Optional final clause** — a quiet observation, no question, no CTA
6. **Hashtag stack of 5–7** at the end

Best fit pillar: Pillar 1 (technical authority) and occasionally Pillar 4 (contrarian). Frequency cap: **no more than 1 per week.** Macro-reaction posts cannibalise the founder-led narrative if they dominate the feed.

## Tom Lee (Fundstrat Capital)

**Status:** Nominated by Cherie 2026-04-28. **LOCKED.**

**Who:** Thomas (Tom) Lee, CIO & Portfolio Manager at Fundstrat Capital, New York. Wharton 1991, CFA 1994. Markets strategist with regular CNBC appearances; high-volume LinkedIn presence; known for bullish equity calls. Communication register is *accessible-strategist*: optimistic, plainspoken, long-only discipline ("Stay long. Stay invested. Skip the market timing mistakes.").

### What Cherie values about Tom Lee (her answer 2026-04-28)

1. **Conviction** — he says what he thinks, plainly, without endless caveats
2. **Discipline framing** — long-term, anti-noise, anti-market-timing
3. **Accessibility** — complex ideas, plainspoken delivery

These three borrow cleanly into Aethelgard. The rest of his public brand (price targets, default bullishness, high-frequency cadence, Wall-Street register) is explicitly not borrowed.

### What to BORROW

#### 1. Conviction without hedging — on principles
When Aethelgard states a *principle* — architectural, accounting, or stewardship — it should land flat, in one sentence, without softening, without caveat, without "in some cases" or "broadly speaking". The reader is intelligent and can handle a stated position.

Examples in Aethelgard register:
- *"Your net-worth file shouldn't live on someone else's server."*
- *"Spreadsheets aren't a system. They're a coping mechanism."*
- *"If you can't answer it in one place, you can't answer it."*

The conviction is on **principles and architecture**, never on outcomes (markets, prices, yields). That distinction is what keeps us out of regulated-advice territory.

#### 2. Discipline framing — anti-noise, anti-market-timing, decade-thinking
Tom Lee's "stay long, stay invested" framing translates directly into Aethelgard's stewardship register. The throughline:

> *Wealth that compounds for decades doesn't reward whoever traded most. It rewards whoever recorded most.*

Use this to anchor:
- Pillar 1 (technical authority) — *"Period locks aren't bureaucracy. They are how a record survives twenty years of edits."*
- Pillar 3 (succession) — *"The records that hold up over decades aren't built in a sprint."*
- Pillar 4 (contrarian) — *"Most wealth-tech sells you speed. Aethelgard sells you a record that still makes sense in 2046."*

Discipline framing is one of the most defensible and brand-aligned moves we have. Reach for it often.

#### 3. Accessibility without dumbing down
Tom Lee can explain a market view to a CNBC audience without losing rigour or talking down to a CFA. That's the standard. For Aethelgard:

- Plain words for the hard concept, never the easy one. *"FX revaluation"* in the title, *"how a euro account is worth a different number of pounds depending on when you measure"* in the body.
- One technical term per paragraph maximum. Define it with a phrase, not a sentence.
- The reader is intelligent. Treat them as such — but don't assume they've read IFRS 9 cover to cover.

### What NOT to BORROW (kept from earlier analysis)

- ❌ Price targets / specific predictions — Aethelgard never forecasts markets, asset prices, or yields
- ❌ High-frequency posting — wrong cadence for the brand
- ❌ Default bullishness — Aethelgard is *neutral on outcome, focused on clarity*
- ❌ Wall-Street trading-floor register — wrong audience flavour
- ❌ AI-sector commentary as a recurring beat

### Composition cue for Claude — "Tom Lee mode"

Triggered when: drafting a Pillar 1 (technical authority) or Pillar 4 (contrarian) post that needs to **state a principle** rather than tell a story or comment on news.

Structure (LinkedIn post, 200–500 words):

1. **Sentence 1** — the principle, stated flat. No "I think". No "perhaps". No softening. *"Spreadsheets aren't a system. They're a coping mechanism."*
2. **Sentence 2–3** — one specific consequence of that principle being true. With a number if possible.
3. **Sentence 4–6** — the discipline frame: why this matters across decades, not quarters. Translate the principle into stewardship.
4. **Sentence 7–9** — the accessibility move: take the hardest technical concept underpinning the principle, render it in plain language, in one paragraph.
5. **Closing** — one sentence that echoes Sentence 1, slightly shifted. Refrain-shaped, not slogan-shaped.

### Tom Lee mode vs. El-Erian mode — when to reach for which

| Situation | Mode |
|---|---|
| A real-world dated event needs commentary (HMRC, BoE, IFRS) | **El-Erian** — cool, hedged, more-interesting-perhaps |
| An Aethelgard *principle* needs to be stated and defended | **Tom Lee** — flat conviction, discipline frame, accessible |
| A founding-story / client vignette | Neither — Cherie's own register, Brian Bacon adjacent |
| A succession piece | Cherie's own register, with Tom Lee discipline framing layered in |

### One refrain-test

The Tom Lee composition cue is also a **refrain test**. After drafting, ask: *what is the one sentence in this post that the reader would screenshot?* If you can't find one, the post is missing the conviction line. Add it.

### Composition cues for Claude — "Verified Investing modes"

#### Explainer mode (matches Pillar 1 — technical authority)
Triggered when: the seed contains a finance concept that the ICP would benefit from understanding more deeply, and a worked example is possible.

Structure (600–1200 words):
1. **Title** — concept name, no clickbait. *"FX revaluation, with a worked example."*
2. **Opening (1 paragraph)** — what the concept is, in plain language, in one sentence; one sentence on why it matters to the ICP.
3. **The mechanic (200–300 words)** — explain the concept itself, with a single specific example (one entity, one currency, two dates, real-looking numbers).
4. **The worked example (200–400 words)** — numbers, dates, journal entries. The numbers are the content.
5. **Why it matters (100–200 words)** — what gets miscounted if this is done wrong; what gets clear if it's done right.
6. **Closing (1–2 sentences)** — Tom Lee mode: a flat principle that lands.

Output also: 1 newsletter excerpt, 1 carousel script, 3 LinkedIn posts spinning off different angles of the same explainer.

#### Schematic mode (a new post format alongside prose posts)
Triggered when: a concept is more clearly explained as a diagram than as prose.

Structure:
1. **Visual** — one clean monochrome or two-tone image. T-account, entity diagram, audit chain, etc.
2. **Caption** — 40–120 words. Three sentences max:
   - Sentence 1: what the diagram shows
   - Sentence 2: why it matters for the ICP
   - Sentence 3: a flat principle (Tom Lee mode close)
3. **No CTA in the post body.**

#### Demystification mode (Pillar 4 — contrarian)
Triggered when: positioning Aethelgard against the £50K–£200K institutional-platform alternative.

Structure:
1. **Open with the gap, anchored in £** — *"For 30 years, the only option for individuals managing serious wealth properly was Addepar at £100K/year, or a family office team."*
2. **Name the wrongness of every other answer** — consumer apps wrong job, cloud accounting wrong scope, enterprise platforms wrong price (this is already on the About page, lifted directly).
3. **State the architectural difference plainly** — not "we're cheaper", but *"we're a different shape entirely"*.
4. **Close** — Tom Lee mode flat principle. *"Institutional accounting, on your laptop. £-not-£100K."*

### What to BORROW

#### 1. Newsletter-as-institution discipline (NOT the length)
Diamandis ships a long-running newsletter — *the discipline is the brand*. Same sender, same day, same structure every issue.

**Important correction (2026-04-28):** Cherie does not read Diamandis's actual emails because **they are too long**. So we borrow the *publication-as-institution* discipline (consistent cadence, sender, structure) — but we explicitly do NOT borrow his length. Aethelgard's newsletter is shorter than Cherie would write if she were trying to match him. See length rules in `ops/style-guide.md` — short discipline is itself a brand asset, and a corrective to the Diamandis miss.

#### 2. Framework-driven thinking — "Aethelgard Frameworks"
Diamandis has the Six Ds, the Abundance Ladder, MTP. These are *durable mental models* readers carry around for years. Aethelgard should build its own small set of named frameworks. Provisional candidates:

- **The Three-Tool Gap** — already on the About page (consumer apps wrong job, cloud accounting wrong scope, enterprise platforms wrong price). Promote this from website copy to a named framework.
- **The Four Pillars of a Record That Survives** — durability, completeness, tamper-evidence, intelligibility-to-a-stranger. (Working title.)
- **The Stewardship Horizon** — the contrast between trader-time (days), founder-time (years), and steward-time (decades). Maps to all three audience pivots.
- **The Successor's Question** — *"What do I own, in one place, today?"* — already implicit; promote to named framework.

Frameworks earn the right to be capitalised proper-noun style only after Cherie has used them in three pieces consistently. Until then, they are working titles, lower-case.

#### 3. Ambitious purpose framing (the "MTP" move)
Every Aethelgard piece, no matter how technical, should have a thread that connects to the bigger purpose. Diamandis writes about a specific quantum technique or a biotech reagent, but every piece ladders up to abundance. Aethelgard writes about period locks or hedge accounting, and every piece ladders up to *"a single, complete, cryptographically verified record of everything you own — in a form that someone else could understand and act on."*

Composition cue: every piece, in the closing 1–3 sentences, lifts the eye from the mechanic to the purpose. Brian Bacon ultimatum-question can do this. Tom Lee flat-principle can do this. The lift is non-negotiable.

#### 4. Specifics-backed optimism
Diamandis says "the future is faster than you think" and then names the technology, the company, the data point, the timeline. Aethelgard's optimism — quiet, decade-spanning — should use the same anchoring. Never "wealth tech is changing" without naming the architectural shift, the cost gap, the specific mechanism.

### Naming note

### What to BORROW

#### 1. First-principles framing
The technique: ignore "how is this normally done?", ask "what does the underlying reality demand?" — and build the answer up from there. Aethelgard's architecture already uses this move: local-first wasn't "the trend in fintech", it was the answer to *what does a record about my entire financial life actually require?* The same move is available across content:

- **Q: "What does an accounting tool need to do?"** — First principles: it needs to balance, be tamper-evident, survive its owner, and produce records a stranger can read. Everything else is decoration.
- **Q: "What does a successor need from your records?"** — First principles: completeness, intelligibility, durability, and someone to authenticate them. Not a portal login.
- **Q: "Why no bank sync?"** — First principles: a sync is a *trust delegation* to a third-party aggregator and a *data leak* to that aggregator's servers. Both violate the architecture.

The technique is most powerful when it produces a non-obvious answer. If first-principles thinking produces the conventional answer, the post probably doesn't need writing.

#### 2. Engineering decisions made explicit
Musk publishes the *why* of every consequential engineering choice — and the *trade-off* he accepted to get there. SpaceX explains why stainless steel over carbon fibre; Tesla explains why a unified casting over a stamped frame. The reader sees the decision being made, not just the result.

For Aethelgard, this maps directly to a category of post Cherie has not yet written but should: **architecture explainers** that name the choice, the alternatives, and the trade-off accepted.

Examples:
- *"Why a SQLite vault and not a JSON file or a custom binary format"*
- *"Why local-first and not optionally-cloud"*
- *"Why WebDAV-only sync and not a bespoke Aethelgard cloud"*
- *"Why Windows first and not Mac"*
- *"Why no bank sync, ever — even when users ask"*
- *"Why a SHA-256 hash chain and not a Merkle tree"*

These pieces accomplish three jobs at once:
- They demonstrate technical depth — feeding Pillar 1 (technical authority)
- They make the architectural choices defensible to scrutiny — useful for partner and press conversations
- They turn what looks like a *limitation* (Windows-only, no bank sync, no mobile) into a *deliberate choice* — feeding Pillar 4 (contrarian / category-definition)

#### 3. "This is hard, we're doing it anyway" honesty
Musk often names the difficulty of what's being attempted, openly. *"This is the hardest manufacturing problem we've ever faced."* The honesty earns trust — *and* it forecloses cheap criticism, because the speaker has already named the problem the critic was about to point out.

Cherie already does this on the About page in places — *"the records people leave their successors are almost universally inadequate"* — but the move can be stronger. Variants for Aethelgard:

- *"Building a double-entry ledger that runs entirely on a laptop with zero telemetry is not the easy way to ship a finance product. It is the right way."*
- *"Local-first means the file is your responsibility. We will not pretend that is a feature for everyone."*
- *"Aethelgard does not sync with your bank. Several thousand competitors do. None of them are correct accounting tools — and that is why."*

The honesty has to be *real difficulty*, not performative humility. The Cramer-anti-rule still applies: no apology theatre, no "I was wrong, here's the new call". This is *naming what's hard about a deliberate choice*, not flagellation.

## Jim Cramer

**Status:** Nominated as anti-reference by Cherie 2026-04-28.

**Who:** Host of CNBC's *Mad Money*. Former hedge fund manager. Public face of theatrical retail-investor stock commentary in the US. Co-founder TheStreet.

### The specific tells that Cherie does not want

| Tell | What he does | Why it's wrong for Aethelgard |
|---|---|---|
| **Theatrical sound effects** | "Booyah!", buzzers, bells, soundboard interruptions, on-screen graphics that flash and explode | Aethelgard's gravitas comes from *plainness*. Theatre cheapens authority. **Banned.** |
| **Catchphrase-driven brand** | "Buy buy buy!", "Sell sell sell!", named lists ("the Charitable Trust", "Cramer's Faves") | Slogan-shaped marketing. Aethelgard refrains are principle-shaped, not slogan-shaped (the difference is whether a peer would screenshot it or roll their eyes). **Banned.** |
| **Hyper-specific buy/sell calls** | Names individual stocks daily, attaches buy/sell verdicts | Aethelgard makes **zero** investment calls — not on stocks, ETFs, crypto, property, anything. Regulated-advice territory and brand-suicide. **Banned.** |

| **Audience-as-fans framing** | "My viewers", "Cramerica", t-shirts and merch | Aethelgard's audience is *clients of the work*, not fans of the founder. The relationship is fiduciary in spirit, even when not in fact. **Banned.** |
| **Apology-then-pivot accountability theatre** | When a call fails, on-air "I was wrong" segments that pivot quickly to a new call | Performative humility. Real accountability looks like *not making the call in the first place*. **Banned.** |

| **Network-TV register** | Loud, fast, confrontational, designed for clip-ability | Aethelgard's register is the conversation in a private banker's office at 11am on a Tuesday. Slow, considered, quiet. **Banned.** |
| **Politics-adjacent commentary** | Frequent crossover into political markets / electoral predictions | Aethelgard avoids political commentary by policy. **Banned.** |

### Composition cue for Claude — the "Cramer check"

Before any Aethelgard post, scan against this list. If **any** Cramer tell is present, the draft fails the publish gate. Specifically:

- ❌ No catchphrases that aren't earned across years of consistent use
- ❌ No buy/sell/hold language about any asset class, ever
- ❌ No bullish or bearish positioning on markets
- ❌ No "I was wrong, here's the new call" reversals (we don't make calls to reverse)

- ❌ No theatrical urgency
- ❌ No "Aethelgardians" / community-as-fandom language
- ❌ No reactive timing — slow beats fast in this brand

The Cramer check and the Dovedi check together give a clean two-pass filter:

- **Dovedi** = property-guru direct-response slop
- **Cramer** = financial-entertainment theatre

Aethelgard's voice sits in the opposite quadrant of both: **slow, plain, observant, principle-stating, conviction-where-architecture-demands-it, neutral-where-markets-are-concerned.**
