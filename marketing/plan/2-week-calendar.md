## WEEK 1 — Monday-seed pillar: Technical authority

### Tuesday — Pillar 1 — Explainer mode (Tom Lee close)
**Working title:** *FX drift: why most net-worth figures are silently months out of date*
**Hook:** *"Most net-worth figures aren't wrong. They're stale."*
**Core idea:** Hardcoded rates go stale; live feeds break silently; historical reporting *requires* a rate table at the right date. One worked example — a euro account at three different dates, three different valuations.
**Length cap:** 400 words.
**Closing direction (Tom Lee mode):** *"A net-worth figure that uses today's rate for last year's amount isn't a measurement. It's a fiction."*
**Hashtags:** `#Accounting` `#IFRS` `#FamilyOffice` `#PrivateWealth` `#FXRevaluation`

**Hook:** *"A spreadsheet you can edit yesterday's entries in isn't a record. It's a draft."*

### Thursday — Pillar 3 — Brian Bacon mode + Diamandis purpose-lift  ⭐
**Working title:** *The conversation no one wants to have*
**Hook:** *"In the last five years, I've watched people I know walk into the conversation no one wants to have. Sometimes for a parent. Sometimes for themselves. Sometimes for a child. The map is never ready."*
**Core idea:** Succession isn't an estate-planning problem. It's a **map** problem. Wills are usually fine. Trustees are usually fine. Structure is usually fine. What is **not** fine is the complete picture of what exists. Brian Bacon move: name the question the reader is reaching for ("we have a will"), dismantle it, reframe. Cherie speaks as someone whose network is living this *now* — first-person observation, not abstract teaching.
**Length cap:** 500 words.
**Closing direction (Brian Bacon ultimatum-question, samaana seed):**
> *"At some point, every record we keep becomes the answer to a question we are no longer here to answer. If you cannot answer it yourself, in one place, today — your successor will answer it for you. Badly."*
**Hashtags:** `#SuccessionPlanning` `#FamilyOffice` `#PrivateWealth` `#Stewardship` `#Estate`
**Confidentiality:** the "people I know" framing must be defensibly broad — no specifics that could identify any individual. Koninklijke NBA confidentiality duty applies.

This piece pairs with the `article-succession.md` cornerstone publishing the same week. The LinkedIn post is the **emotional door**; the cornerstone is the room behind it.
