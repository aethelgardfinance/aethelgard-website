# Content Pillars — the four lanes Aethelgard publishes in

Rotate weekly. Never deviate. If a draft can't trace back to one of these, do not publish it.

---

## Pillar 1 — Technical authority

**Job:** Establish the moat. Demonstrate finance-grade competence no SaaS marketing team can fake.

**Topics:**
- IFRS 9 hedge accounting — for the founder without a CFO
- Deferred tax workings — what your accountant won't explain
- Multi-currency FX revaluation
- Intercompany elimination in family structures
- Period locks: why your records aren't records without them
- Accruals and prepayments for a holdco
- Fixed asset depreciation methods compared
- The double-entry test — three questions to ask any "accounting" tool

**Format:** Short technical posts (LinkedIn) + cornerstone explainers (blog).

**Cadence:** ~40% of all output. The largest pillar.

**Voice:** Authoritative, plainspoken, never condescending. The reader is intelligent and already operates in this world.

---

## Pillar 2 — Founding story / client vignettes

**Job:** Earn trust through specificity. Show the human reality behind the technical product.

**Topics:**
- "There's a conversation I've had dozens of times…"
- The folder client — anonymised
- The spreadsheet that worked once and never again
- The accountant who asked the question nobody answered
- The exit that exposed the structure
- The inheritance that revealed the gap

**Format:** Narrative LinkedIn posts (300–600 words) + occasional newsletter long-form.

**Cadence:** ~25% of output.

**Voice:** Conversational opening, builds to a quiet declarative line, never preachy. **Always anonymised.** Never reverse-engineerable to a real client.

**Constraint:** Cherie's Koninklijke NBA confidentiality duties are binding. When in doubt, abstract further.

---

## Pillar 3 — The succession angle

**Job:** Reach the family-principal ICP. Land the most emotionally durable framing in the category.

**Topics:**
- "At some point — for all of us — someone else will need to understand what we own."
- What your executor will actually need
- The records you'd want to inherit vs. the records people leave
- The conversation that should happen while it can
- Estate planning data is not estate planning advice
- Why "my accountant has it" is not the answer

**Format:** Sparing, occasional, emotionally weighty posts.

**Default cadence:** ~10% of output, **maximum one per month**. Reaching for this pillar more often dilutes it.

**Calibration override (2026-04-28, Cherie's signal):** for the next quarter (Q2 2026), Pillar 3 may run at *up to two posts per month* — driven by Cherie's direct observation that her network is actively living the succession problem this year. People in her connections are experiencing it for parents, for themselves, and for their children. Pillar 3 retains its rarity-as-power principle even at this elevated rate; if anything posted feels formulaic or preachy, the cadence drops back to monthly immediately.

**Voice:** Quiet, declarative, never manipulative. The frame works because it is true; ruined by overuse. **First-person observation of relevance to the founder's own network is permitted and powerful** ("In the last five years, I've watched people I know…") — provided no individual is identifiable. Koninklijke NBA confidentiality duty is binding.

## Pillar 4 — Contrarian takes on wealth-tech

**Job:** Define category boundaries. Position Aethelgard against the alternatives without naming them clumsily.

**Topics:**
- Why Xero is not an accounting system for your life
- Why £50K/year wealth platforms fail HNW individuals
- Local-first is a feature for the user, not the vendor
- Bank sync is a feature for the SaaS company, not for you
- Why budgeting apps cannot become wealth apps
- The privacy cost of cloud-first accounting
- Subscription lock-in is a tax on people who already paid

**Format:** Punchy LinkedIn posts; longer-form contrarian essays for blog.

**Cadence:** ~25% of output.

**Voice:** Direct, not sneering. Argues from architecture, not from disliking competitors. Names mechanisms, not brands (most of the time).

## Distribution per piece

A single seed produces:

- **1 blog post** (cornerstone if Pillar 1, narrative if Pillar 2/3, opinion if Pillar 4)
- **3 LinkedIn posts** — different angles on the same seed
- **1 LinkedIn carousel** (10 slides)
- **1 newsletter section**
- **5–7 reply snippets** for engaging in comments

That's the leverage move. One seed, six channel-ready outputs, ~60 minutes of Cherie's edit time.
