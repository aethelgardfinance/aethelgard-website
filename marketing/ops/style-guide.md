# Style Guide — Aethelgard public communication

Operational rules. Mechanical. Apply to every published piece.

---

## Mechanics

- **British English.** "organisation", "centre", "behaviour", "favour". Even when writing for US audiences.
- **Currency:** GBP primary. £ symbol, no space. "£99/year" not "GBP 99 per year."
- **Numbers under 10 spelled out** in narrative ("three folders"). Numerals in technical/financial contexts ("£3M–£40M").
- **Em-dashes:** unspaced — like this. Not double hyphens.
- **Quotation marks:** "double" for direct quotes; 'single' for nested quotes.
- **Dates:** ISO in metadata (2026-04-28); "28 April 2026" in narrative.

## Length discipline (corrected 2026-04-28)

Cherie has named *length* as a reason she stops reading some of the writers she otherwise admires (Diamandis emails; Brahma Kumaris classes). Length is therefore an explicit Aethelgard discipline, not a default-to-long. **Short is the discipline.**

Caps by format (treat as upper bounds, not targets):

| Format | Range | Hard cap |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn post (default) | 150–500 words | 700 |
| LinkedIn post (deliberately long-form) | 500–800 words | 1000 |
| LinkedIn carousel | 10 slides, 25–50 words each | — |
| LinkedIn Schematic caption | 40–120 words | 150 |
| Newsletter section (single concept) | 400–800 words | 1200 |
| Newsletter issue (total) | 600–1200 words | 1500 |
| Blog cornerstone | 1500–2500 words | 3000 |
| Explainer (Verified-Investing-mode) | 600–1200 words | 1500 |
| Architecture explainer (First-principles-mode) | 800–1500 words | 2000 |
| Founding-story / vignette | 300–600 words | 800 |

Two further rules:

1. **One concept per piece.** Multi-concept pieces are the BK-class failure mode. If a draft contains more than one durable idea, split it into two drafts and ship over two weeks.
2. **If unsure, cut.** If you cannot decide between two paragraphs, cut both and write one shorter one. The reader's time is the most expensive resource Aethelgard spends.

## Banned in all drafts

- AI-tells:
  - "It's not just X, it's Y"
  - "In the age of [whatever]"
  - "Imagine a world where…"
  - "Picture this:"
  - Excessive em-dash density (more than one per paragraph average)
  - "Whether you're a [X], a [Y], or a [Z]…" tricolons
- Marketing slop:
  - "unlock", "leverage", "game-changer", "revolutionary"
  - "solution", "platform", "ecosystem" (without specific anchor)
  - "we are excited to", "thrilled to", "humbled to"
- Empty modifiers:
  - "truly", "really", "actually", "literally"
  - "very" + adjective (use a stronger adjective)
- LinkedIn-isms:
  - "Hot take:" / "Unpopular opinion:"
  - "👇 thread"
  - "Save this post"
  - "Tag someone who needs this"
  - Performative humility ("I'll never forget the day my mentor told me…")
- **Financial-entertainment / TV-host tells** (the "Cramer check" — see `brain/voice-references.md`):
  - Catchphrases or slogans that aren't earned across years of consistent use
  - Buy / sell / hold language about any asset class, ever
  - Bullish or bearish positioning on markets
  - Prediction reversals ("I was wrong, here's the new call") — we don't make calls to reverse
  - Exclamation marks in body copy (titles get one max, sparingly)
  - Theatrical urgency, "must-see", "you need to know NOW"
  - Audience-as-fandom language ("Aethelgardians", "my viewers", merch register)
  - Loud-fast network-TV cadence — Aethelgard's register is the conversation in a private banker's office at 11am on a Tuesday
- **Property-guru / direct-response tells** (the "Dovedi check" — see `brain/voice-references.md`):
  - "No fluff, no hype, no BS" disclaimers — earn it, don't announce it
  - Scarcity countdowns: "X tickets left", "price triples soon", strikethrough numbers
  - Fear-of-loss framing: "those who don't adapt will lose £X"
  - Yield, profit, or return promises in any register ("6-figure profits")
  - Self-anointed rankings ("the UK's #1 X", "the leading…")
  - Guilt-trip unsubscribe copy ("we'd hate to see you go", warning emoji on opt-out)
  - "Valuable" as self-description ("our valuable content")
  - Headline-Case-Everything in body copy
  - Venue or postcode used as credibility prop
  - First-name cold opener with no prior relationship
  - Sad-face emojis (or any emoji) in commercial copy

## Required for every published piece

- Spell-checked
- Read aloud once before publishing
- Contains at least one specific, concrete detail (a number, a place, a moment)
- Ladders up to the spine sentence in `brain/positioning.md`
- Picks ONE of the three pivots (A/B/C) — does not blend
- Closes with a clear next step or a deliberate silence (never "what do you think?" if the silence is more powerful)

## Forbidden topics

- Politics, current affairs commentary
- Other founders by name unless complimentary and consented
- Specific client identifiers — even oblique ones
- Crypto price predictions
- Any speculation about HMRC enforcement actions
- Anything that could be construed as regulated financial advice ("you should invest in…", "this is tax-deductible because…")

When near a forbidden topic, the rule is: **describe principles, not actions.** "How families typically structure X" — fine. "You should structure your X like this" — not fine.

## SEO mechanics for blog content only

- Target keyword in H1
- Target keyword in first 100 words
- 2–3 internal links to other Aethelgard pages
- 1 external link to authoritative source (HMRC, IFRS, Companies House, Koninklijke NBA)
- Meta description 140–155 characters, action-oriented
- All images have alt text
- URL slug 3–5 words, lowercase, hyphenated
